## How does this benefit the community?

LabOps Agent prevents diagnostic delays in clinical laboratories by predicting reagent stockouts before they happen, rather than reacting after testing has already stopped.

In Argentina, ~900 PAL-accredited clinical labs serve as the primary diagnostic access point for millions of patients, many of whom rely on public health infrastructure. When a lab runs out of a reagent mid-cycle, every patient waiting on that test loses days — sometimes weeks — of diagnostic certainty, directly delaying treatment decisions.

This has a disproportionate impact on vulnerable populations:

- **TSH (thyroid) testing** — critical for pregnant women, where untreated thyroid disorders are linked to developmental risks for the fetus, and for elderly patients managing chronic conditions.
- **Hemograma (complete blood count)** — the primary screening tool for anemia in children, a widespread and treatable condition if caught early.
- **Ionograma and other core panels** — used for emergency and chronic disease monitoring across all age groups.

By predicting stockouts days in advance instead of discovering them after the fact, LabOps Agent gives lab staff time to reorder before testing capacity is interrupted — directly protecting continuity of care for the patients who depend on these labs most.

Every prevented stockout means uninterrupted diagnostic access. For TSH: thyroid screening for pregnant women. For hemograma: anemia detection in children.
